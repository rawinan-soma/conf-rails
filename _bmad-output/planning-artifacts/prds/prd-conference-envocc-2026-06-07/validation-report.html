ENVOCC Conference Room Booking System — Validation Report

_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/prds/prd-conference-envocc-2026-06-07/prd.md
Fair

This is a tight, internally consistent PRD that knows exactly what it is: a security-driven full replacement of a fragmented two-app booking + registration system for a single organization. Its strongest assets are done-ness clarity and downstream usability — FRs are granular, ID-stable, and cross-referenced through a coherent role model that resolves the hard role edge cases (FR-094 RBAC, FR-063 room-deactivation cascade, F11 internal vs. external registration). On the rubric alone it sits in "Good" territory: no broken dimension, one thin dimension (scope honesty), one high finding (NFR-003).

The grade drops to Fair because the adversarial pass materially shifts the picture: it surfaced 6 critical and 9 high implementation-readiness gaps. The headline reliability claim ("100% / 0 double-bookings", NFR-002) is currently both unimplementable and untestable as written — FR-011→FR-004 is a textbook check-then-insert race with no atomic guarantee (C-1, C-2). Time-sensitive logic (registration close FR-034, reminders FR-037) names no timezone or fire time (C-3); email-delivery failure is unhandled despite the confirmation email being the registrant's only artifact and cancellation path (C-4); duplicate registration by the same email is never prevented (C-6); and "Attended" (FR-035) is a dead enum no FR ever sets (H-6). These are gaps of under-specification rather than structural defects — exactly the material the architecture and correct-course phases exist to resolve — but they should be closed before downstream work leans on them. Note also the stale identity: frontmatter still reads project_name: conference-envocc inside a project called conf-rails (M-2).

Decision-readiness

adequate

The PRD states decisions as decisions, not considerations: meal-type ownership is pushed to the registrant (FR-021), admins are stripped of approval authority in three places (§3, FR-094, Constraints), and the resent-confirmation flow names the security trade-off it accepts (FR-047). Where it is merely adequate: there is no Open Questions section and no [NOTE FOR PM] callouts, yet FR-095 references "resolves OQ-3" — an open-question artifact that does not exist in the document, so the resolution is unverifiable in context. It also smooths over the uncapped-registration vs. room-capacity tension (FR-032 / FR-061) without flagging it as a contestable trade-off.

Done-ness clarity

adequate

Adversarial review — implementation-readiness

review-adversarial-general.md

Not implementation-ready as written. The PRD reads well but defers nearly every hard distributed-systems decision (concurrency, time, delivery, dedup) to the implementer, and its flagship reliability claim is currently both unimplementable and untestable. Severity counts: 6 critical, 9 high, 11 medium, 7 low (33 total). The 6 critical + 9 high are listed below; the 18 medium/low findings are in review-adversarial-general.md.

critical

C-1 — Conflict detection has no concurrency/race-condition spec

FR-004, FR-011, NFR-002, Goal 4
"Check availability then insert" is a textbook time-of-check-to-time-of-use race. The "100%" guarantee is unimplementable without a locking strategy and untestable — no concurrent-case test, no loser-of-race UX.
Fix: Mandate an atomic guarantee at the persistence layer (DB exclusion/overlap constraint), specify the loser-of-race UX, and define the concurrency test that validates "100%".
critical

C-2 — "Overlapping time slots" never defined; boundary semantics unspecified

FR-004
Is 09:00–10:00 in conflict with 10:00–11:00? Half-open vs. closed intervals give different answers. No buffer rules; zero-duration / end-before-start not rejected.
Fix: Define intervals as half-open [start, end), state adjacent bookings do not conflict, add validation for end > start and minimum duration.
critical

C-3 — Timezone & date-boundary behavior unspecified across all time-sensitive logic

FR-033, FR-034, FR-037, FR-093, NFR-006
"End of that date" — in whose timezone? "1 day before" — 24h before start, or a fixed clock time on the prior day? Off-by-a-day bugs that ship silently.
Fix: Declare a canonical timezone (Asia/Bangkok). Define "end of date" as 23:59:59 in that zone and the reminder fire time precisely (e.g., 09:00 the prior calendar day).
critical

C-4 — Email delivery failure handling completely absent

FR-013, FR-016, FR-042, FR-080, FR-082
SMTP-only with no managed retry layer, yet zero spec for SMTP down/reject/timeout. The confirmation email is the registrant's only artifact and carries the sole cancellation link — silent failure leaves them with no record and no recovery.
Fix: Add retry policy + failure visibility + queue/status; decouple registration commit from email send; define dead-letter/alert and registrant recovery.
critical

C-5 — Manual close / cancel of in-flight registrations undefined

FR-034b, FR-015, FR-063, FR-104
What happens to a registration in-flight when the organizer closes/cancels, or an admin deactivates the room? No ordering rule → registrations attached to cancelled events, emails for events that no longer exist, counts that disagree with reality.
Fix: Make close/cancel transactional; reject any registration not committed before the close timestamp with the standard "closed" message; define behavior for registrations committed just before cancel.
critical

C-6 — Duplicate registration by the same email never addressed

FR-041, FR-042, FR-047, FR-101, FR-105
FR-047 assumes one registration per email, but nothing prevents the same email registering many times — inflating counts, catering aggregation, and the sign-in sheet, and making resend ambiguous. Compounded by an organizer self-registering both internally and externally.
Fix: Enforce uniqueness on (event, email) for external registrations and reconcile internal vs. external by the same person; if duplicates are intentional, define which link FR-047 resends.
high

H-1 — Untestable/vague NFR thresholds with no verification method

NFR-001, NFR-003, NFR-004
"Normal organizational load" has no number; "securely" is unverifiable; "no critical/high vulnerabilities" needs a defined scanner and CVSS cutoff.
Fix: Quantify load (p95 ≤ 3s with N users, M-booking dataset); name the scanner/standard + CVSS threshold; replace "stored securely" with concrete requirements.
high

H-2 — "100% accuracy" / "0 incidents" is not a measurable success metric

Goal 4, Success Metrics, NFR-002
Zero observed incidents is lagging and unfalsifiable — you can only ever fail it; a single unobserved race passes undetected.
Fix: Convert to a verification criterion (K simultaneous overlapping submissions → exactly one success, K-1 rejections); keep the production "0" as a monitoring KPI, not an acceptance gate.
high

H-3 — Self-cancellation token security & lifetime unspecified

FR-043, FR-044, FR-092, NFR-001
No token entropy, expiry, single-use vs. reusable, or post-cancellation state. A weak/non-expiring token lets anyone cancel or enumerate registrations.
Fix: Specify cryptographically-random ≥128-bit tokens, single-use cancellation semantics, expiry tied to event lifecycle, and the post-cancellation state.
high

H-4 — Resend anti-enumeration claimed but other surfaces leak

FR-047 vs. FR-051, FR-072
Good intent, inconsistent threat model: timing leaks, dashboard registrant count, and an undefined "already registered" response on the submit path undermine the neutral-acknowledgement posture.
Fix: Require the registration submit path to also not distinguish "already registered" to anonymous users; require constant-time-ish resend behavior.
high

H-5 — Auto-close / reminder scheduling mechanism & reliability undefined

FR-033, FR-037, FR-082
Time-triggered jobs with no spec for how they fire, what happens if the job server is down at trigger time, or whether reminders are idempotent — risky given fragile SMTP-only delivery.
Fix: Specify scheduling approach, missed-run recovery (catch-up vs. skip), and idempotency (at most one reminder per registrant per event).
high

H-6 — "Attended" status has no defined transition mechanism

FR-035, FR-036, FR-038
The data model includes "Attended" but nothing sets it — no check-in FR despite a sign-in sheet and QR code. A dead enum an implementer cannot build.
Fix: Add an attendance-marking FR (organizer marks on the registrant list, or QR-based self check-in), or remove "Attended" from FR-035.
high

H-7 — Catering toggled off after registrations exist — meal-data fate undefined

FR-021, FR-022, FR-023, FR-041a
Toggling catering off (then maybe on) leaves collected meal selections and aggregated counts in an undefined state; re-enabling creates registrants with null meal types inside an aggregation that assumes a value.
Fix: Define meal-type data lifecycle across toggle transitions and the aggregation/display behavior for registrants with no meal type.
high

H-8 — Organizer profile contact propagation conflicts with read-only per-event contact

FR-040, FR-095
Contact is pulled live from a profile the user can edit later. If the organizer changes their phone after a booking, does the published registration page change retroactively? Snapshot vs. live is never stated.
Fix: State whether the contact is snapshotted at booking creation or resolved live, and the consequence for already-published pages.
high

H-9 — Internal-user-as-both-organizer-and-registrant edge cases under-specified

FR-094, FR-100, FR-105, FR-035
Organizer-as-registrant appears in a list they manage; do they receive the cancellation email they themselves trigger? Does admin read-all override the "cannot see registrant list" restriction when an admin views another's event to register?
Fix: Add a short conflict-resolution matrix for organizer-as-registrant and admin-as-registrant/viewer, including self-notification suppression.
